# Acceptable Use of IT Systems, Internet and Email Policy (Morocco)

> **General reference – Use of company IT resources and professional tools**  
> This policy is inspired by Moroccan legal principles relating to employer responsibility, protection of company assets, confidentiality and appropriate use of electronic communications in the workplace.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define the rules governing the use of the Company’s IT equipment, systems, internet access, email and collaboration tools.  
It aims to ensure secure and responsible use of these resources, protect the Company’s information and reputation, and prevent misuse or unlawful activities.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees of the Company in Morocco;  
- Interns, temporary workers, consultants and service providers who use the Company’s IT resources;  
- All IT resources and services provided by the Company, including but not limited to:  
  - Computers (desktops and laptops), smartphones and tablets;  
  - Networks and Wi‑Fi;  
  - Business applications, intranet and collaboration platforms;  
  - Professional email accounts and instant messaging tools.

## 3. General principles

- IT resources are provided **primarily for professional purposes**.  
- Limited personal use may be tolerated, as long as it remains reasonable, does not interfere with work, does not generate additional costs and does not violate this policy or applicable laws.  
- Users must act in good faith and with common sense when using the Company’s tools, particularly when their actions may have security, legal or reputational consequences.

## 4. Use of equipment and accounts

1. **Individual responsibility**  
   - Each user is responsible for the equipment and access accounts assigned to them (computer, phone, email address, login, etc.).  
   - Devices must be locked when left unattended, even for a short period.

2. **Passwords and authentication**  
   - Passwords must be kept **strictly confidential** and must not be shared with colleagues or third parties.  
   - Users must choose strong passwords and change them regularly, in accordance with the Company’s security rules.  
   - Any suspicion of password compromise must be reported without delay to IT support.

3. **Installation of software**  
   - Only software authorised or validated by the IT department may be installed on Company devices.  
   - Installing unapproved software, especially from untrusted sources, is prohibited.

## 5. Use of internet and online services

- Internet access is provided to support work activities: research, communication with clients and partners, access to professional tools, etc.  
- Users must not access, download or distribute content that is illegal, offensive, discriminatory, violent or pornographic.  
- The use of peer‑to‑peer file-sharing networks or sites that clearly present security risks is prohibited.  
- Users must be vigilant when browsing and avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown files.

## 6. Use of professional email and messaging

1. **Professional use**  
   - Professional email addresses and messaging tools are intended for business communications with colleagues, clients, partners and other contacts.  
   - The tone and content of messages must remain professional, respectful and consistent with the Company’s values.

2. **Prohibited uses**  
   - Sending or forwarding messages containing offensive, discriminatory or harassing content;  
   - Mass mailing or unsolicited messages (spam) that are not related to work;  
   - Transmitting confidential information to persons not authorised to receive it;  
   - Using professional email for personal business, political or religious campaigns, or other non-professional causes.

3. **Attachments and links**  
   - Caution must be taken when opening attachments or clicking on links, particularly when the sender is unknown or unexpected.  
   - Suspicious emails (phishing, scams, etc.) must be reported to IT support or the security contact.

## 7. Confidentiality and protection of information

- Users must protect the confidentiality of information obtained in the course of their work (commercial, financial, technical data, personal data, etc.).  
- Sensitive documents must not be stored on unprotected devices or shared via non-secure channels.  
- When using cloud services, file-sharing tools or external applications, users must comply with the Company’s security and confidentiality rules and use only approved solutions.

## 8. Monitoring and logs

- Within the limits of applicable laws, the Company may implement **logging and monitoring** of IT systems (connection logs, email traffic metadata, web access logs, etc.) in order to:  
  - Ensure the security of systems and data;  
  - Detect and prevent misuse, incidents or attacks;  
  - Comply with legal and regulatory obligations.  

- Monitoring is carried out in a proportionate manner and is not intended to be used for arbitrary or intrusive surveillance.  
- Users are informed that their use of Company resources may be recorded and analysed for security and compliance purposes.

## 9. Incidents and suspected misuse

- Any incident likely to affect the security or integrity of systems or data (virus, phishing, loss or theft of equipment, unauthorised access, etc.) must be reported immediately to IT support or the designated security contact.  
- Any suspected misuse of IT resources (for example, use of illegal sites, unauthorised software, circumvention of security measures) must also be reported.

## 10. Sanctions

Failure to comply with this policy may lead to disciplinary measures in accordance with the Company’s internal rules and Moroccan labour law, which may include:

- Verbal or written warning;  
- Restriction of access to certain tools or systems;  
- Temporary suspension of functions;  
- Termination of employment or contractual relationship in serious cases.

## 11. Awareness and training

- The Company may organise training and awareness sessions on IT security, acceptable use of tools and protection of information.  
- Users are encouraged to ask questions and seek advice if they are unsure whether a particular use is compliant.

## 12. Review of the policy

This Acceptable Use of IT Systems, Internet and Email Policy is reviewed periodically and in particular when:

- There are significant changes in applicable laws or regulations;  
- The Company deploys new technologies or tools;  
- Security incidents or new types of misuse are observed.

In the event of conflict between this policy and mandatory legal provisions, **the legal requirements shall prevail**, and the policy shall be updated accordingly.
