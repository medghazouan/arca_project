# Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination Policy (Morocco)

> **General reference – Protection of dignity and non-discrimination at work in Morocco**  
> This policy is inspired by Moroccan labour law provisions prohibiting harassment and discrimination at work, as well as international principles of equal treatment and respect for human dignity.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a working environment in which all individuals are treated with dignity and respect, free from any form of harassment or discrimination.  
The Company has **zero tolerance** for harassment or discriminatory behaviour and undertakes to prevent, identify and deal with such conduct without delay.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees of the Company, regardless of their position, contract type or seniority;  
- Interns, temporary workers, consultants and service providers;  
- Any person present on Company premises or participating in activities organised by the Company (meetings, events, business trips, etc.).

It covers behaviours occurring:

- On Company premises (offices, sites, common areas, etc.);  
- During working hours or on working time;  
- Outside the workplace when the behaviour is linked to work (business trips, professional events, exchanges on professional tools, etc.).

## 3. Definitions

1. **Harassment**  
   - Harassment is any repeated behaviour (words, acts, gestures, writings, images, etc.) that has the purpose or effect of degrading working conditions, undermining the rights and dignity of a person, affecting their physical or mental health, or jeopardising their professional future.  
   - Harassment may be **moral (psychological)** or **sexual** in nature.

2. **Sexual harassment** (examples)  
   - Unwanted remarks or comments of a sexual nature;  
   - Insistent proposals with a sexual connotation;  
   - Inappropriate physical contact or gestures;  
   - Displaying or sending sexual content in a professional context.

3. **Moral (psychological) harassment** (examples)  
   - Repeated humiliating remarks, insults or mockery;  
   - Social isolation, exclusion from meetings or information without valid reason;  
   - Excessive or unfair criticism, disproportionate sanctions or threats;  
   - Imposing impossible objectives or constantly changing instructions with the intention of destabilising the person.

4. **Discrimination**  
   - Discrimination is any unfavourable treatment of a person based on criteria prohibited by law or contrary to the principles of equality and diversity (for example: origin, gender, age, disability, religion or beliefs, marital status, political opinions, union activity, etc.).  
   - This may relate, among other things, to hiring, pay, training, promotion, disciplinary action or termination of employment.

## 4. Principles and commitments of the Company

The Company undertakes to:

- Prohibit any form of harassment or discrimination in its workplaces;  
- Promote a culture of respect, inclusion and open dialogue;  
- Inform employees of their rights and responsibilities under this policy;  
- Provide channels for safe and confidential reporting of incidents;  
- Investigate reported facts impartially, promptly and with respect for all parties;  
- Take appropriate corrective and disciplinary measures when harassment or discrimination is established.

## 5. Responsibilities of employees and managers

- **All employees** must:  
  - Treat colleagues, managers, subordinates, clients and partners with respect;  
  - Refrain from any behaviour that could constitute harassment or discrimination;  
  - Report, in good faith, any situation they witness or experience which appears to be contrary to this policy.

- **Managers** have a particular responsibility to:  
  - Set an example through their own behaviour;  
  - Ensure that their teams work in a climate of respect and cooperation;  
  - React promptly to any signal of harassment or discrimination brought to their attention;  
  - Support employees who express concerns and direct them to the appropriate contacts (HR, management, etc.).

## 6. Reporting and handling of complaints

1. **Reporting channels**  
   Any person who considers themselves a victim or witness of harassment or discrimination may report the situation through one or more of the following channels (adapt to the Company’s organisation):

   - Directly to their line manager;  
   - To the Human Resources department;  
   - To a designated ethics or compliance contact, if applicable;  
   - Through any confidential reporting mechanism implemented by the Company.

2. **Protection of complainants and witnesses**  
   - No employee shall be sanctioned or suffer reprisals for making a report in good faith, even if the facts are not ultimately characterised as harassment or discrimination.  
   - The identity of the persons involved and the information collected are handled with the utmost confidentiality, within the limits of the investigation.

3. **Investigation and decisions**  
   - Reported facts are examined promptly, objectively and impartially.  
   - The investigation may include interviews, document review and any other necessary measure.  
   - If harassment or discrimination is established, appropriate measures are taken, up to and including disciplinary sanctions in accordance with the internal rules and applicable law.

## 7. Sanctions

Depending on the seriousness of the facts, sanctions may include, in particular:

- Verbal or written warning;  
- Disciplinary suspension;  
- Demotion or reassignment;  
- Termination of employment contract;  
- Termination of a contract with a service provider or external partner.

Sanctions are decided in compliance with the procedures and guarantees provided by Moroccan labour law.

## 8. Awareness and training

- The Company may organise awareness and training sessions on harassment, discrimination and respectful behaviour at work.  
- These actions aim to help employees recognise unacceptable behaviour, understand the effects of harassment and discrimination, and adopt good practices.

## 9. Review of the policy

This Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination Policy is reviewed periodically and in particular when:

- There are changes in applicable legislation in Morocco;  
- The Company observes new types of risks or receives significant feedback from employees;  
- Serious or repeated incidents are identified.

In the event of any conflict between this policy and mandatory legal provisions, **the legal requirements shall prevail**, and the policy shall be updated accordingly.
