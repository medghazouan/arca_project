# Anti-Corruption, Gifts and Hospitality Policy (Morocco)

> **General reference – Anti-corruption principles and business integrity**  
> This policy is inspired by Moroccan legal provisions on corruption and bribery, as well as international standards on integrity, transparency and the prevention of undue advantages in business relationships.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear rules to prevent **corruption**, **bribery** and inappropriate **gifts and hospitality** within the Company’s activities in Morocco and abroad.  
The Company applies a **zero-tolerance** approach to corruption and expects all employees and partners to behave with integrity and honesty.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees of the Company, regardless of their position or contract type;  
- Managers, directors and members of governance bodies;  
- Interns, temporary workers, consultants and service providers acting on behalf of the Company;  
- Business relationships with clients, suppliers, intermediaries, public authorities and any other third party.

It covers all Company activities, whether conducted in Morocco or in other countries.

## 3. Definitions

- **Corruption**: offering, promising, giving, requesting or accepting an undue advantage (financial or otherwise) to influence the actions of a person in the performance of their duties.  
- **Bribe**: any payment, gift or benefit offered with the intention of obtaining an improper advantage (contract award, favourable decision, confidential information, etc.).  
- **Facilitation payment**: small, unofficial payment made to speed up or secure a routine action (for example, obtaining a permit or service to which the Company is legally entitled).  
- **Gifts and hospitality**: meals, invitations, travel, accommodation, tickets to events, promotional items or any other benefit offered or received in a professional context.

## 4. General principles

The Company undertakes to:

- Prohibit any form of corruption or bribery, whether active or passive;  
- Refuse facilitation payments, even if they are customary in certain contexts;  
- Ensure that gifts and hospitality remain **reasonable, proportionate and transparent**, and never influence or appear to influence a business decision;  
- Comply with applicable anti-corruption laws and regulations in Morocco and in any other country where the Company operates.

## 5. Prohibited practices

The following practices are strictly prohibited:

- Offering, promising or giving money or any valuable item with the intention of influencing a decision or obtaining an undue advantage;  
- Requesting or accepting such advantages from third parties;  
- Paying facilitation payments to public officials or private persons;  
- Concealing or misrepresenting payments or transfers of value in Company records;  
- Using intermediaries, agents or partners to carry out corrupt acts on behalf of the Company.

## 6. Gifts and hospitality

1. **Acceptable gifts and hospitality**  
   Gifts and invitations may be acceptable if they are:

   - Of **low value** and reasonable in nature (for example, promotional items, modest meals in the context of a business relationship);  
   - Occasional and not repeated in a way that could create a sense of obligation;  
   - Offered openly and transparently, in accordance with local customs and Company rules;  
   - Not linked to a specific decision in progress (award of a contract, tender, negotiation, etc.).

2. **Prohibited or sensitive situations**  
   Gifts and hospitality are prohibited or must be refused when:

   - They are offered with the apparent or explicit intention of influencing a decision;  
   - Their value is excessive or disproportionate in relation to the business context;  
   - They are offered during or shortly before/after a tender, negotiation or decision-making process;  
   - They are directed at public officials or representatives of public bodies, except under very strict conditions and with prior approval.

3. **Approval and declaration**  
   - The Company may set **value thresholds** beyond which any gift or invitation must be declared or approved by management or the compliance function.  
   - Employees must follow internal procedures for declaring gifts and hospitality received or offered, where such procedures exist.

## 7. Relations with public officials

Particular caution must be exercised in relations with:

- Public servants and state officials;  
- Representatives of public institutions, local authorities or state-owned enterprises;  
- Political figures or candidates for public office.

Any advantage, gift, invitation or payment involving a public official must comply with applicable legal restrictions and, where required by internal rules, be subject to prior approval.

## 8. Third parties, agents and intermediaries

- The Company conducts appropriate **due diligence** on intermediaries, agents, consultants and partners who may interact with clients, suppliers or public authorities on its behalf.  
- Contracts with third parties must include clauses prohibiting corruption and requiring respect for this policy or equivalent standards.  
- Any warning sign (unusual commissions, pressure to make cash payments, refusal to disclose beneficiaries, etc.) must be investigated and, if necessary, lead to the termination of the relationship.

## 9. Accounting and financial records

- All payments and financial transactions must be recorded **accurately and transparently** in the Company’s accounts.  
- It is forbidden to create false invoices, off-book accounts or any other mechanism intended to conceal payments or benefits.  
- Controls may be carried out to ensure compliance with accounting and anti-corruption rules.

## 10. Reporting concerns and protection against retaliation

- Any employee who has doubts or becomes aware of possible corruption, a suspicious payment or inappropriate gift or hospitality must report the situation through the channels provided (manager, HR, compliance contact, whistleblowing mechanism, etc.).  
- Reports made **in good faith** will not result in disciplinary action or retaliation, even if the suspicion is not ultimately confirmed.  
- The identity of the person making the report and the persons involved is treated confidentially, within the limits necessary for investigation.

## 11. Sanctions

Failure to comply with this policy may lead to disciplinary measures, which may include:

- Written warning or reprimand;  
- Suspension or demotion;  
- Termination of the employment contract;  
- Termination of a contract with a service provider or partner;  
- Where appropriate, reporting to competent authorities in accordance with legal obligations.

## 12. Training and awareness

- The Company may organise training sessions and awareness campaigns on anti-corruption, gifts and hospitality rules.  
- Employees in positions particularly exposed to corruption risks (purchasing, sales, public tenders, negotiations with public authorities) may receive specific training.

## 13. Review of the policy

This Anti-Corruption, Gifts and Hospitality Policy is reviewed periodically, and in particular when:

- Anti-corruption legislation evolves in Morocco or internationally;  
- New activities or markets increase exposure to corruption risks;  
- Incidents, audits or risk assessments highlight the need for improvements.

In the event of conflict between this policy and mandatory legal provisions, **the legal requirements shall prevail**, and the policy shall be updated accordingly.
